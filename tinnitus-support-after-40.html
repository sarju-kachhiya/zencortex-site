Why Tinnitus Becomes More Noticeable With Age

Illustration: Ringing sensation associated with age-related tinnitus

The inner ear contains delicate hair cells responsible for detecting sound vibrations. These cells depend heavily on healthy circulation and cellular protection.

As we age, microcirculation may decline and oxidative stress may increase. This combination can contribute to auditory sensitivity and ringing sensations.

The Connection Between Blood Flow and Hearing

The inner ear relies on extremely small blood vessels to deliver oxygen and nutrients. When blood flow is optimized, auditory nerve signaling is better supported.

Supporting circulation is one of the most overlooked strategies in maintaining hearing clarity after 40.
